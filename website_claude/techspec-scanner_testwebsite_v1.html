Product

What TechSpec Scanner does.

An iPhone app for AI-assisted identification of mechanical and industrial components. Capture, analyse, verify, and export in a structured workflow.

Step 01

Take or upload component photos

Guided photo slots walk you through the right angles — overview, markings, side profile, and scale reference. Or upload from your camera roll.

  • Overview, markings, profile, and scale capture slots
  • Import from photo library or file storage
  • Works with dirty, worn, or partially visible parts
  • Multiple images per scan session
Step 02

Add measurements & receive AI analysis

Enter known measurements or context notes before submitting. Photos and context are sent to the backend for Gemini processing.

  • Optional measurement and context fields
  • Backend-protected Gemini processing
  • Structured output: type, characteristics, visible evidence
  • Confidence score and uncertainty flags in every result
Step 03

Review evidence & apply corrections

Every result surfaces the visible evidence used, uncertainty flags, and recommended physical checks. Add manual corrections and save verified parts locally.

  • Visible evidence summary with every result
  • Recommended checks and measurements
  • Technician note and manual override support
  • Save verified components to your known-parts library
Step 04

Export technical reports

Generate structured reports with photos, scan data, AI analysis, and technician notes. Export locally, share with colleagues, or archive for maintenance records.

  • PDF — human-readable report with photos
  • JSON — structured data for integration
  • CSV — spreadsheet-compatible parts list
  • Local backup file for offline archiving
  • English, German, French, and Spanish interface

AI Disclaimer

AI limitations & safe use.

TechSpec Scanner is an AI-assisted tool. This page explains what that means and how to use the app responsibly.

TechSpec Scanner provides AI-assisted suggestions, not certified engineering verification. Results can be incomplete, incorrect, or misleading. Always verify with physical measurements, visible markings, manufacturer documentation, and qualified technical judgment before acting on any result.

What TechSpec Scanner is

TechSpec Scanner assists with first-pass identification of mechanical and industrial components from photographs using Google Gemini. It is a starting point, not a conclusion.

What TechSpec Scanner is not

TechSpec Scanner is not a certified testing or measurement tool. It does not perform physical measurement, material analysis, or standardised inspection. It should not be the sole basis for:

  • Safety-critical engineering or installation decisions
  • Purchasing or procurement without independent verification
  • Repair or maintenance where incorrect identification could cause harm
  • Engineering design or load-bearing applications

How the AI can be wrong

  • Ambiguous appearance. Many components look similar — bearings, bushings, and washers can be indistinguishable without measurement.
  • Photo quality. Poor lighting, angle, dirt, wear, or missing markings reduce accuracy significantly.
  • Hallucination. Language models can produce plausible but incorrect part numbers or specifications.
  • Missing context. Without physical dimensions or system context, the AI works from incomplete information.
  • Non-standard parts. Components that deviate from standard specifications will not be identified correctly.

How to verify a result

  • Measure key dimensions with calibrated instruments (calipers, micrometer)
  • Read all visible stamped or printed markings
  • Cross-reference with manufacturer datasheets or standards tables
  • Consult a qualified engineer or technician where safety is involved

Confidence scores

Confidence scores reflect the model's internal estimate — not an independently validated accuracy measurement. A high confidence score does not guarantee a correct identification. Uncertainty flags should always be acted on.

No liability

TechSpec Scanner and its developers accept no responsibility for decisions made on the basis of AI-generated identification results. The user is responsible for verifying all results before acting on them.

Privacy Policy

How your data is handled.

TechSpec Scanner is built on a local-first principle. Most data never leaves your device.

Summary

TechSpec Scanner stores scan history, known parts, projects, preferences, and test logs locally on your device. Data is only transmitted to external services when you actively request an AI analysis. The app does not use advertising, does not track behaviour, and does not require an account.

Data stored on your device

  • Scan history and session records
  • Photos captured or uploaded during sessions
  • Technician notes and manual corrections
  • Known-parts library entries
  • Projects and grouped scan collections
  • App preferences and settings
  • Scan accuracy test logs

You can delete all locally stored data at any time from within app settings.

Data sent to the backend during analysis

When you submit a scan for AI analysis, the following is sent to the TechSpec Scanner backend:

  • Photos captured or uploaded for that scan session
  • Any measurements or context notes added before submission
  • An anonymous install identifier (usage counting only — not linked to your identity)

Google Gemini processing

AI analysis uses the Google Gemini API. Photos and context are transmitted to Google's API infrastructure. Google's handling is governed by the Google Privacy Policy. The Gemini API key is stored only on the backend server — never in the app or on your device.

What is not collected

  • Your name, email, or personal identity (unless you contact support)
  • Location data
  • Device contacts, calendar, or unrelated files
  • Advertising identifiers
  • Behavioural analytics or usage tracking

There are no subscriptions, payment systems, or cloud accounts in the current version.

Anonymous install identifier

A random anonymous install ID is generated at first launch for usage counting only. It is not linked to your identity, device name, or Apple ID.
